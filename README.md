## Insights
* Attrition rate is 16.12% that indicates that the company is losing talent at a concerning rate.
* With a total of 62 attrition cases, Sales Executives show the highest attrition by job role. This indicates issues specific to this department, such as compensation, workload, career progression opportunities, or training opportunities.
* Business Travelling is a major driver of employee attrition (65.82% for "Some Travel"), significantly impacting both single (54.01%) and married (33.33%) ****employees.  This highlights a critical work-life balance challenge associated with travel, regardless of marital status, demanding a closer look at travel policies and potential alternatives.
* The analysis indicates that the majority of employees are under 30, and this younger demographic shows a higher likelihood of leaving due to longer commutes, suggesting a need to address distance-related attrition to retain this potential asset.
* Besides, other reasons leading to the rise of attrition include low average job satisfaction (3.43) and low training opportunity (2.01). This highlights widespread dissatisfaction and a lack of investment in employee growth.

## Recommendation
* Implement comprehensive feedback mechanisms (exit interviews and pulse surveys) to diagnose and address core reasons for leaving.
* Conduct a thorough Sales department review encompassing compensation, work load, and career pathways to enhance retention.
* Mitigate travel impact through optimized schedules, enhanced support, and technology to improve traveler well-being.
* Offer flexible work options and explore commute assistance to retain young talent.
* Enhance the L&D strategy with increased investment and diverse programs to foster employee growth.
